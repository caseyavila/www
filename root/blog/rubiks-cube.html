Solving a Rubik's cube from first principles

August 2024

Right after the end of this past school year, I decided to go back home and take a short break from everything. I just completed my third year of college, and school, on top of a part time job (and all the other things college students are up to) really adds up. While I was home, I came across some old childhood toys, including a few Rubik's cubes. Seeing them almost surprised me; it had been such a long time since I had even thought of a Rubik's cube.

As is common among (certain types of) kids, I had a Rubik's cube phase when I was younger (think 4th or 5th grade). I was never the fastest at solving it, but I remember having some friends that were really into it. I also wasn't an uber-genius of a child, so ten-year-old me resorted to YouTube to learn how to solve to cube. With enough practice, I was able to learn the beginner's method and get solves in around one minute. Still, one decade later, a question remained:

Could I have solved the Rubik's cube from scratch?

Obviously, I had spoiled the puzzle for myself as a kid.
